Peloton

A recall landed during the company's highest-visibility moment. We ran the read that shaped the brand and product response, on a timeline the standard research cycle was not built for.

01The situation

The decision in front of the leadership team was not "how do we apologize." It was "what do we say, in what order, to whom, and what do we change about the product story while we say it." The window was days, and every public move would be read as a signal about whether the company could be trusted.

Standard research timelines could not produce a defensible read in the time the decision actually had. They needed a sharp outside view, fast, that the internal team could stand behind in a room with the board.

02What we did

We ran a crisis-research protocol built for the clock: a baseline read of where the brand actually stood, a fast pass on how customers and prospects were interpreting the moment, and message testing on the responses under consideration. Not a 60-page appendix. The two or three things that would change the call.

  • Accelerated brand-health baseline
  • Voice-of-customer pulse on the live moment
  • Message and posture testing across response options
  • A single read, routed to the people deciding
03Where it landed

The read did more than get presented. It set the sequence of the public response and reframed the product narrative around what customers were actually worried about. We stayed with it through the decision, not just up to it, so the finding arrived in the room where the call was made, in a form the team could act on that week.

It then seeded a standing brand-health program so the next move would not need a fire drill to inform it.
